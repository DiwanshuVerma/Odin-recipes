Delicious Shahi Paneer

Shahi Paneer is a rich and creamy Indian dish that features paneer cooked in a luscious gravy made with cashews, cream, and aromatic spices.

Here's a simple recipe for making Shahi Paneer at home:

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked in warm water for 15-20 minutes
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream (you can use heavy cream or malai)
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar (optional, for a hint of sweetness)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a blender, make a smooth paste of soaked cashews along with chopped tomatoes, grated ginger, and minced garlic. You can add a splash of water if needed.
  2. Heat ghee or vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, green cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Saut√© for a minute until the spices release their aroma.
  3. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent and slightly brown.
  4. Now, pour in the cashew-tomato paste and cook it on medium-low heat, stirring frequently. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the gravy, and it thickens.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt to the gravy. Mix well and cook for another couple of minutes.
  6. Gradually add the milk and fresh cream to the gravy, stirring continuously to combine everything well. If the gravy becomes too thick, you can adjust the consistency by adding a little water.
  7. Add the paneer cubes to the gravy and gently mix them in, ensuring they are well coated with the sauce. Let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the paneer to soak in the flavors.
  8. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it over the gravy for a delightful aroma.
  9. If you prefer a slightly sweet taste, you can add a tablespoon of honey or sugar at this stage (optional).
  10. Finally, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Your Shahi Paneer is now ready to be served! Enjoy this creamy and flavorful dish with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
