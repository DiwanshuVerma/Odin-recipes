Mashala Chai

Masala chai, also known as spiced tea, is a popular and aromatic Indian beverage made with a combination of tea, spices, milk, and sweeteners.

Here's a simple recipe to make a delicious cup of masala chai:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 teaspoons black tea leaves (or 2 tea bags)
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 3-4 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 2-3 whole cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick or 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of black peppercorns (optional)
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Instructions

  1. Crush the whole spices: Using a mortar and pestle or the back of a spoon, lightly crush the green cardamom pods and cloves to release their flavors.
  2. Boil water and spices: In a saucepan, add the water, crushed spices, grated ginger, cinnamon stick, black peppercorns (if using), and nutmeg (if using). Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes. This allows the spices to infuse their flavors into the water.
  3. Add the tea: Add the black tea leaves or tea bags to the saucepan and simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes. If you prefer a stronger tea, you can simmer it for a little longer.
  4. Add milk and sugar: Pour in the milk and add the sugar to the saucepan. Stir well and let the chai come to a gentle boil. Keep an eye on it to prevent overflowing.
  5. Strain and serve: Once the chai has reached your desired strength and consistency, turn off the heat. Using a fine mesh strainer or a tea strainer, pour the chai into cups to remove the spices and tea leaves.

Enjoy your masala chai: Your masala chai is now ready to be enjoyed. Serve it hot and savor the rich, aromatic flavors.
